Karnataka Faces Surge in Dog Bite Cases

Karnataka is seeing a sharp rise in dog bite incidents, with 3.1 lakh cases reported from January to August 2025. Bengaluru is the most affected, accounting for over 1.2 lakh cases. Health authorities warn of risks of rabies and stress vaccination, sterilization, and public awareness. Municipal corporations are stepping up stray dog management, while NGOs support vaccination drives and community education. Citizens are urged to avoid confrontation with stray dogs, report aggressive animals, and follow medical advice after bites. The government also plans stricter penalties for pet abandonment to curb the stray dog population.
